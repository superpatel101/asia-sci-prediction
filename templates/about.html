Overview

This application provides machine learning-based predictions for spinal cord injury outcomes at discharge, based on patient data collected at admission. Two Random Forest models were trained on data from the National Spinal Cord Injury Statistical Center (NSCISC) database:

  • Motor Score Predictor: Predicts ASIA motor score at discharge (0-100 scale)
  • Impairment Grade Predictor: Predicts ASIA Impairment Scale grade at discharge (A/B/C/D/E)

Model Performance

Motor Score Prediction Model

  • R² Score: 0.8122 (explains 81.2% of variance)
  • RMSE: 11.7 points
  • MAE: 7.6 points
  • Training Data: 10,543 patients
  • Algorithm: Random Forest Regressor (200 trees)

Impairment Grade Prediction Model

  • Accuracy: 75.4%
  • F1-Score (Weighted): 0.7433
  • AUC (Weighted): 0.9178
  • Training Data: 15,053 patients
  • Algorithm: Random Forest Classifier (200 trees)

Expected Recovery by Grade

Based on training data analysis:

  • Grade A (Complete): Average improvement +3.8 ± 9.3 points, 43% improve
  • Grade B (Sensory Incomplete): Average improvement +14.9 ± 18.8 points, 79% improve
  • Grade C (Motor <50%): Average improvement +25.6 ± 20.1 points, 91% improve
  • Grade D (Motor ≥50%): Average improvement +12.3 ± 12.9 points, 83% improve

Grade C patients show the best motor recovery potential.

Most Important Features

The models use 26 admission-time features. The most influential are:

For Motor Score Prediction:

  1. ASIA Motor Score at Admission (42.9%) - Most predictive factor
  2. ASIA Impairment Grade at Admission (19.7%)
  3. Functional Independence Score (8.5%)
  4. Neurological Level (6.7%)
  5. Mechanical Ventilation (5.4%)

For Impairment Grade Prediction:

  1. ASIA Impairment Grade at Admission (38.3%) - Most predictive factor
  2. Days from Injury to Rehab (14.2%)
  3. Mechanical Ventilation (7.5%)
  4. Neurological Level (7.4%)
  5. Age at Injury (7.2%)

Limitations

  • Motor score model explains 81% of variance - 19% remains unexplained
  • Individual outcomes can vary significantly from predictions
  • Models trained on North American population (NSCISC database)
  • Grade B classification has lower accuracy (F1=0.56)
  • Predictions are statistical estimates, not guarantees
  • No data leakage - uses admission features only

Clinical Use

These models are designed to assist with:

  • Early patient and family counseling
  • Setting realistic expectations and goals
  • Rehabilitation planning and resource allocation
  • Research and quality improvement initiatives

Important: Predictions should complement, not replace, clinical judgment. They provide statistical likelihoods based on historical data.

Data Source

Models were trained on the National Spinal Cord Injury Statistical Center (NSCISC) database, which contains comprehensive data on spinal cord injury patients from specialized centers across North America.
